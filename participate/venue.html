About NITK Surathkal

The National Institute of Technology Karnataka (NITK), Surathkal is one of India's premier technical institutions, established in 1960. Spread across a lush 295-acre campus on the shores of the Arabian Sea, NITK is consistently ranked among the top engineering institutes in the country.

The campus is a unique blend of academic excellence and natural beauty — bordered by a serene beach on one side and the Western Ghats on the other. With state-of-the-art research facilities, modern conference halls, and warm hospitality, NITK provides an ideal setting for an international conference of ICDCN's stature.

The conference will be hosted at the Lecture Hall Complex (LHC), equipped with large auditoriums, seminar halls, and modern AV infrastructure, capable of comfortably accommodating all conference tracks.

Life at NITK

NITK Surathkal offers a one-of-a-kind campus experience that conference attendees will find memorable long after the event ends.

The Beach

NITK's own private beach stretches along the campus. The iconic lighthouse standing tall on the rocks is one of the most photographed spots on the entire Konkan coast.

Green Campus

Lush greenery, coconut groves, and scenic walking paths make the campus a refreshing retreat. The sunsets over the Arabian Sea from campus are truly spectacular.

Food & Culture

Coastal Karnataka cuisine — fresh seafood, neer dosa, mangalorean curries — is a culinary adventure. On-campus and nearby restaurants cater to all preferences.

Accommodation

Guest houses within campus and a range of hotels in nearby Mangalore (17 km) are available. Detailed accommodation information will be shared closer to the conference.

How to Reach

NITK Surathkal is well connected to the rest of India by air, rail, and road.

By Air

Mangalore International Airport (IXE)

The nearest airport, approximately 22 km from NITK. Direct flights operate from Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, Delhi, Hyderabad, and several Gulf cities. Prepaid taxis and app cabs are available at the airport.

Calicut International Airport (CCJ)

Approximately 180 km from NITK. A good alternative with strong connectivity to Gulf destinations. Buses and taxis to Mangalore are readily available.

Taxi from Mangalore Airport to NITK takes approx. 35–45 minutes. Fare ₹600–₹900.

By Train

Surathkal Railway Station

The closest station, just 2 km from NITK. Several trains on the Konkan Railway and other routes stop here. Auto-rickshaws and taxis are available.

Mangalore Central / Mangalore Junction

The main railway hub, approximately 18 km away. Well-connected to Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, Delhi, Goa, and Kerala. Local buses, autos, and cabs to NITK are easily available.

Recommended trains: Matsyagandha Express (Mumbai), Netravati Express (Thiruvananthapuram), Mangala Lakshadweep (Delhi).

By Road

From Bangalore

Approximately 350 km via NH 75. KSRTC and private luxury buses operate overnight and daytime services. Journey takes about 7–8 hours.

From Goa

Approximately 200 km via NH 66 (Coastal Highway). Scenic coastal drive or direct bus services available. Journey takes about 4–5 hours.

From Mangalore City

NITK is 17 km from Mangalore city centre. City buses (Route 35A), autos, and cabs connect regularly throughout the day.

NITK address for navigation: NITK Surathkal, NH 66, Srinivasnagar, Surathkal, Mangalore – 575025, Karnataka.

Local Attractions

Mangalore and its surroundings offer a rich mix of beaches, temples, heritage sites, and natural wonders — perfect for a day trip before or after the conference.

Panambur Beach

One of Mangalore's most popular beaches, just 5 km from NITK. Known for water sports and the scenic fishing harbour.

~5 km

Kadri Manjunath Temple

A historic 10th-century temple in Mangalore city, famous for its bronze statues and coastal architecture style.

~18 km

St. Aloysius Chapel

A magnificent Jesuit chapel renowned for its elaborate ceiling and wall murals painted by Italian artist Moscheni.

~20 km

Kudremukh National Park

A stunning UNESCO-listed biodiversity hotspot in the Western Ghats, perfect for trekking and wildlife spotting.

~100 km

Bekal Fort

A magnificent 17th-century fort in Kerala with a panoramic ocean view, one of the largest and best-preserved forts in Kerala.

~65 km

Dharmasthala

A famous pilgrimage town on the banks of the Nethravathi river, known for its Manjunatha temple and charitable institutions.

~75 km

Murudeshwar Temple

A coastal Shiva temple famous for its towering 20-storey gopura and a massive Shiva statue overlooking the sea.

~80 km

Malpe Beach & St. Mary's Island

A scenic beach town with boat rides to the iconic hexagonal basalt rock formations of St. Mary's Island (Coconut Island).

~55 km
